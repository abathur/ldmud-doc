1 lfuns {#driver_topics_lfuns}
2 ============================
3 An lfun is a LPC function within an object which is public and can be called by other objects. In OO terms, lfuns are "methods" which you can send "messages" to.
4 
5 Calling lfuns is done by using efun::call_other(), which takes as arguments the object in which the lfun is to be called, the name of the lfun to be called in the object, and additional and optional arguments.
6 
7 An example looks like this:
8 
9 ~~~{.c}
10 call_other(drink, "QueryShort");
11 
12 ~~~
13 This call may also be written as:
14 
15 ~~~{.c}
16 drink->QueryShort();
17 
18 ~~~
19 This means `call_other(object, "function", args...)` can also be written as `object->function(args...)`. The second form is preferred as it is easier to read.
20 
21 Some lfuns have a special meaning for the LPC driver, because they are applied by the interpreter instead from an LPC object. To distinguish those, they are called ">>:directory:`applied`<< lfuns".
